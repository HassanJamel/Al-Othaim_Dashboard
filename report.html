Dataset Overview

The dataset comprises daily OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) records sourced from Tadawul via Yahoo Finance.

📋 Column Definitions

ColumnTypeDescription
DateDatetimeTrading date on the Saudi Stock Exchange (Tadawul).
OpenFloatPrice at which the stock opened for the trading day (SAR).
HighFloatMaximum price reached during the trading session (SAR).
LowFloatMinimum price reached during the trading session (SAR).
CloseFloatFinal adjusted closing price of the trading day (SAR).
VolumeIntegerTotal number of shares traded during the day.

📌 Note on Adjusted Prices

Close prices are backward-adjusted for historical corporate actions (stock splits, dividends) to enable continuous time-series analysis without artificial gaps.

Key Analytical Findings

Insights derived from the exploratory data analysis conducted in the Jupyter notebook (New_App.ipynb).

📈 Trend Analysis

  • Growth Phase (2010–2015): The stock rose from ~0.76 SAR to its all-time high of 68.54 SAR, driven by aggressive retail expansion across Saudi Arabia.
  • Correction Phase (2015–2020): A significant drawdown occurred in line with oil price volatility and broader Saudi market corrections.
  • Stabilization Phase (2020–2026): Post-COVID recovery followed by gradual normalization, with the stock trading near 6.66 SAR at the latest data point.

📊 Distribution Insights

  • Daily returns follow a leptokurtic distribution — heavier tails than a normal distribution, indicating occasional extreme price swings.
  • Volume distribution is right-skewed, with intermittent spikes corresponding to earnings announcements and market events.
  • The average daily price range (High - Low) was 1.85% of the closing price.

🔗 Correlation & Relationships

  • Open, High, Low, and Close prices exhibit near-perfect multicollinearity (correlation > 0.99), typical of daily OHLC data.
  • Volume shows a weak negative correlation with price, suggesting sell-offs tend to attract higher trading activity.
  • Moving average crossovers (50-day SMA vs 200-day SMA) historically preceded trend reversals by 2–4 weeks.
